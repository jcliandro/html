Issues, the building blocks of any project

Not sure who's doing what by when? In Jira Software, issues make it easy to track individual pieces of work through completion.

In Jira Software, you can break big ideas down into small, bite-size pieces of work called issues. An issue could represent a task, a bug, a feature request, or any type of action your team needs to complete.

Watch issues video

Got the basics down? Here are some tips from Jira Software product managers on how you can use issues to make project planning seamless:

  • Ivan Teong | Product Manager

    Issues contain fields that capture a lot of information about a piece of work, like assignee or due date. You can make it easier to find the info you need by pinning the most important fields to the top of an issue!

  • Tushar Bhatia | Senior Product Manager

    Take advantage of Jira's builtâ€‘in issue hierarchy to break down large bodies of work (epics) into smaller tasks (issues: stories, tasks, bugs) to more accurately represent your team's work!

  • Liron Deutsch | Group Product Manager

    Keep your team focused by configuring issue types to only display the most relevant information. For example, you can hide specific fields on 'tasks' if they're only relevant to 'bugs'.
